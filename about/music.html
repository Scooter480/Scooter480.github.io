Music

I didn't really care about music until high school. Around then I started to discover and appreciate music for the first time. I started my spotify playlist that I still use to this day, and started listening to albums. Some albums, such as Hybrid Theory really resonated with me at the time, and others, such as Enter the Wu Tang were highly regarded classic albums. I explored a lot of 90s hip hop at the time, and later listened to some of the best from the 2000s and 2010s as well. I listened to a lot of classic, progressive, and alternative rock as well. I still listen to a lot of this music, although I'm trying to branch out a little.

As I got into music, I started to amass a small collection of physical music as well. Most of my music was bought at Flipside Records in Berkely, MI. I prefer to get CDs, as they're generally cheaper, have better liner notes, and I can play them at home and in my car, as well as rip them to play on my computer. I have some vinyl records as well, which are fun because you get to watch them spin around and whatnot. I have some cassettes, but I don't really look for prerecorded cassettes, and usually make mixtapes instead. I've even made mixtapes for a friend before, since his car only has a cassette deck and the radio doesn't work.

To listen to music, I have a stereo system that I've slowly amassed over the years. At the heart is a Denon DRA-55V AV receiver. Attached are some Jensen speakers I got for $10 at a garage sale: 2 on the left, 2 on the right, and a subwoofer. The turntable is an Audio Technica AT-LP60 with a diamond tip eliptical stylus. It's pretty good especially for the price. I'm hardly an audiophile so I don't really care to upgrade it, it sounds good to me. It has a usb port so I can digitize any records I get, which is nice if they aren't on streaming services and I want to listen on the go. The CD player is acutally a Toshiba DVD player, which my family has had for ages. We use the Xbox for DVDs now, so I snagged the old one. I also listen to CDs in my car. The tape deck is a JVC KD-V22 from 1983, purchased at Flipside. It has Dolby C noise reduction, which is pretty nice. I use this deck to make the afformentioned mixtapes. I also have a Raspberry Pi 3b hooked up to it, which can be streamed to via Airplay or Spoitfy, which is nice if I want to listen to anything that I don't own physically. I might get an upgraded DAC for it later.

I also make music occasionally, although it's not that good. It's mostly just for fun, although I occasionally collaborate with friends, such as Lil Leezy. I use FL Studio, and I'm a big fan of the Korg Triton synth. I only make instrumentals, I can't sing and certainly can't rap. You can check out my SoundCloud here if you're interested.
