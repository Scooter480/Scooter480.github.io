Anti-Flag - The Lies They Tell Our Children

Review Date: 06 January 2023

I wish the album was half as good as the album cover. It's a pop-punk album, and the genre has been fairly lacking recently to my knowledge, considering the most notable albums in pop-punk this decade have been from MGK of all people. This album isn't doing the genre any favors, as while I can agree with the anti-war and anti-capitalist politics on the record, the songwriting and lyrics still leave a lot to be desired. The guitar sounds generic, as do the vocals, and I can't remember a melody from any of the songs. Punk generally isn't the most complex genre, but it's usually not this bland at least. Overall Lies They Tell Our Children leaves a lot to be desired, and you can find music that's similar in focus and in sound done significantly better elsewhere. For a group that's apparently been making music since the 90's, I expect better.

2/10, Favorite song: Imperialism, Least favorite song: Victory or Death (We Gave 'em Hell)
